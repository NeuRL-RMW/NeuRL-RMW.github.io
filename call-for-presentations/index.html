Call for Presentations

We are excited to invite PhD students and postdoctoral researchers to present their published works or ongoing research as spotlight presentations and posters at our upcoming workshop on [Workshop Title]. The spotlight presentations and posters at this workshop aim to provide a platform for young researchers to showcase their cutting-edge work, share their insights, and foster vibrant discussions within the [Field/Theme] community.

We encourage submissions from researchers at various stages of their academic journey, from early-stage PhD students to recent postdocs. This is an excellent opportunity to receive valuable feedback, network with experts and peers in the field, and gain visibility for your research.

Presentation Formats

  • Spotlight Presentations. Selected researchers will have the chance to deliver spotlight talks, providing concise and impactful overviews of their work. Spotlight presentations will be part of focused sessions, highlighting key findings and contributions to the field. Presenters will have the undivided attention of the audience, creating a dynamic and engaging atmosphere.
  • Poster Presentations. Presenters will have the opportunity to showcase their research in a visual and interactive format. Posters will be displayed in a dedicated poster hall, providing an engaging environment for discussions and knowledge exchange. Attendees will have ample time to explore the posters and interact with presenters during the designated poster session.

Submission Guidelines

  • Submissions should include an abstract (PDF | Max file size: 10 MB) that provides an overview of the research topic, key objectives, methodologies, and preliminary or expected results.
  • Please specify your preferred presentation format (poster or spotlight) in the submission. Feel free to include the publication venue of your work as a part of submission.
  • Use this form for your submission.
  • Please note that all presentations should be relevant to the workshop theme and align with the conference's code of conduct.
